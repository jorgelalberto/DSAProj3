Alachua County Social Services
  • Address: 218 SE 24th St., Gainesville, FL 32641
  • Phone: (352) 264-6750
  • Time: Lunes - Viernes de 8:30 de la mañana hasta las 5 de la tarde.
  • Requirements: Must be an Alachua County resident for the past 30 days. The office will make referrals to a separate location for eye exams and to receive eyeglasses. Call for more information. Services provided: Provides vision exams and eyeglasses at low-cost.
Helping Hands Clinic
  • Address: 509 NW 1st St., Gainesville, FL 32601
  • Phone: (352) 519-5542
  • Time: Monday 4:00 pm - 7:00 pm Patient signup begins at 2:30 pm
  • Requirements: Cannot be currently insured, including Medicaid and Medicare. May not be under indigent care or receive any income. This service is primarily geared toward people that are currently homeless. The initial in-person intake is required to determine if a patient is below 200% of poverty guidelines and determine. Services provided: Helping Hands Clinic collaborates with University Opticians of Gainesville to provide homeless individuals with eye exams and prescription glasses.
Equal Access Clinic Eye Care
  • Address: HealthStreet, 2401 SW Archer Rd, Gainesville, FL 32608
  • Phone: (352) 580-0393
  • Time: Our Eye Clinic is offered on the last Tuesday of every month at 6 pm and located at HealthStreet, 2401 SW Archer Rd, Gainesville, FL 32608.
  • Requirements: Appointments are required for this clinic so if you are interested, please call us at (352) 580-0393 at least 2 days prior to the date you want to attend. Eye exams will be conducted at this clinic to test for diabetes, glaucoma, etc.
